Moti Mahal
Moti Mahal is a restaurant.[2] After the partition of India in 1947,[3][4] the Moti Mahal in Delhi was founded by Kundan Lal Gujral, Kundan Lal Jaggi and Thakur Dass as one of the first restaurants to introduce the Punjabi cuisine to the rest of the world.
Delhi Durbar
The Delhi Durbar was an Indian imperial-style mass assembly organized by the British at Coronation Park, Delhi, India, to mark the succession of an Emperor or Empress of India.
Punjab Grill
In Ancient India, Maharaja Ranjit Singh was bestowed with the title of Sher-e-Punjab which meant – The Lion of Punjab for his bravery and courage. Punjab grill is best North- Indian restaurant in Delhi NCR.
The Big Chill
The Big Chill Cafe is a chain of cafes located in New Delhi, the capital of India. The cafe is owned by Aseem Grover and Fawzia Ahmed. The cafe started out in 2000 with its first outlet in East of Kailash, an area in South Delhi.
